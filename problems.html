Problems

Work gets stuck for predictable reasons.

Work sits in approval. Decisions take longer than they should. The same people keep fixing things that should have worked the first time.

These are not separate problems. They are the same pattern showing up in different places. Too much work and too much decision authority are sitting in the wrong parts of the organization.

What this actually looks like inside organizations

A contract sits for days waiting on approval over something that takes minutes to fix. A decision everybody understands still has to climb before anything moves. Work leaves one team and shows up incomplete in the next. A strategic plan gets announced and then quietly stalls.

None of those feel like the same problem when you are inside them. They are. They repeat because the structure underneath them has not been fixed.

Where these problems usually start

Approval chains get layered on and never cleaned up. Decision authority stays too high even as organizations grow. Roles look clear on paper but break in real work. Teams hand work off without carrying enough context forward. A lot of strategy gets built on top of those problems instead of fixing them first.

Each one feels separate when you are dealing with it. From the outside, they usually trace back to the same pattern. Work and authority are piled into the wrong places, so everybody else spends their time waiting, fixing, following up, or cleaning up.

These problems feed each other

A bad approval path slows decisions down because too much authority is trapped in one lane. Unclear roles create duplicate work because nobody trusts the ownership line. Broken handoffs force downstream teams to rebuild what should have arrived ready to move. A strategic plan that depends on all of that still gets announced like the hard part is over.

That is why these pages belong together. You can start with the problem that looks the most familiar, but you should not assume it will stay in one lane for long.

What I am looking for when someone brings me one of these problems

I am not looking for the best abstract label. I want one live sequence people are already tired of dealing with. A contract that always sits. A decision that always climbs. A handoff that keeps breaking. A plan everyone says matters that still runs into the same wall once the meeting ends.

That is enough to start seeing where the structure is putting too much weight in one place and where the rest of the organization is paying for it.
