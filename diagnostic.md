    <h1>The IVA Diagnostic</h1>
    <p>
      Every organization feels the same pressure even when the context looks different. The friction, the load, the slow decisions, and the heroic effort that keeps everything moving are not personal and not unique. They are structural.
    </p>
    <p>
      The IVA Diagnostic is the first process that shows leaders the architecture underneath their experience. It reveals why the work feels heavier than it should, why excellence depends on individual effort, and why the same problems return no matter how many tools or plans you have tried.
    </p>
    <p>
      IVA is grounded in more than a century of research. Financial accounting systems were created for external reporting in the industrial era. Over time they became internal performance regimes. This shift created predictable distortions. IVA exposes these distortions and shows how they shape your daily reality.
    </p>
  </section>

  <!-- WHAT YOU ARE FEELING -->
  <section style="margin-bottom: 48px;">
    <h1>What You Are Feeling and Why</h1>

    <p>
      If you are here, it is because you have built something that works, often better than it should. You have solved problems faster than the structure can adapt. You have carried load the system should have absorbed. You have kept the operation moving through judgment, experience, and endurance.
    </p>

    <p>You may feel:</p>

    <ul>
      <li>decisions that should be simple taking longer than they should</li>
      <li>work bouncing between people because ownership is unclear</li>
      <li>teams stretched thin even when performance is high</li>
      <li>coordination that depends on the right people being available</li>
      <li>problems that disappear and then return in a new form</li>
      <li>leaders carrying work the system should absorb</li>
      <li>success depending on individual effort instead of structure</li>
    </ul>

    <p>
      These are not management issues. These are not culture issues. These are not people issues. These are structural signals that appear in every organization that has outgrown the architecture it inherited.
    </p>
  </section>
