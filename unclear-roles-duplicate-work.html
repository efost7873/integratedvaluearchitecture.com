Problems | Roles

When nobody owns it cleanly, the work gets done twice or not at all.

Two people think they own it. Or nobody does. Or one person is responsible for the outcome while someone else still has the ability to block the work.

Work overlaps, gets redone, or just sits there while people figure out who is supposed to move it.

What unclear roles actually look like in real work

A document gets drafted in two places because nobody knows who owns it. An email gets copied to six people because nobody trusts the ownership line. A task gets finished, then reopened because someone else thought it was theirs.

The same person gets pulled back in to fix things because everyone trusts them more than the structure. Work that should move forward gets dragged sideways instead.

That is not a communication issue. That is a broken ownership line.

Where the time actually goes

People spend time checking who has it. Asking if it is done. Reworking something that should have stayed with the next person.

None of that shows up cleanly on a timeline. It just makes everything take longer than it should.

Why this keeps happening

Roles look clear on paper but break in real work. Responsibility sits in one place while control sits somewhere else. People build workarounds instead of fixing the line.

Over time, the organization stops trusting the structure and starts relying on individuals to keep things moving.

How this connects to other problems

Unclear roles feed approval bottlenecks because nobody knows who can actually move the work. They slow decisions because authority gets pushed upward. They break handoffs because nobody carries ownership across teams.

This is one of the places where everything else starts to stack.
