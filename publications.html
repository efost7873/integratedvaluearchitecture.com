Beyond Financial Dominance

Author: Evan Micheal Foster

Platform: SSRN

Abstract ID: 6050034

Location: Springfield, Illinois

This paper identifies a structural condition common across sectors. Financial accounting systems originally created for external reporting gradually became the dominant internal governance logic. The result is architectural distortion rather than simple budget pressure. The paper clarifies how this shift occurred and outlines the governance consequences that follow when one ledger becomes the primary definition of value.

Availability: The abstract is publicly available on SSRN. Full-text availability depends on SSRN posting status.

What Happens When Small Business Growth Outpaces Capacity

Author: Evan Micheal Foster

Platform: Medium

Format: Public essay

This essay examines a common small business failure pattern. Financials can remain strong while capacity quietly erodes. When only money is measured consistently, human limits stay informal and tradeoffs remain implicit. Over time, financially rational decisions accumulate into operational fragility.

Availability: Publicly available on Medium.

Always Over Capacity

Format: Book manuscript

Status: In development (Expected 2026)

This manuscript examines why work keeps rolling downhill inside organizations. It traces structural overload to governance architecture instead of culture or personality. The book builds a shared language for recognizing structural distortion without assigning blame to any single role.
