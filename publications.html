Published and Public Research

Beyond Financial Dominance

Author: Evan M. Foster

Platform: SSRN

Abstract ID: 6050034

This paper names a structural pattern that most organizations recognize but rarely address directly. It clarifies how financial accounting gained practical control over internal priorities, then traces the governance consequences that follow when one ledger becomes the whole story.

Working Papers and Manuscripts

Always Over Capacity

Format: Book manuscript

Status: In development

This manuscript is written for leaders, operators, and boards who live inside structural overload. It shows how work keeps rolling downhill when governance architecture forces decisions into gaps that nobody owns, then builds a shared language for recognizing the pattern without turning any role into the villain.

Federal Tax Policy and Financialization

Format: Doctoral research

Status: Ongoing

This research examines how federal tax policy reinforces financialization across sectors and how those incentives propagate into governance structure, reporting behavior, and resource distribution.
