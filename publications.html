Publications

This page includes published work and active research by Evan Micheal Foster, founder of Integrated Value Architecture LLC in Springfield, Illinois.

The work focuses on a simple question that keeps showing up in different forms: why do the same problems keep coming back even after people try to fix them?

Published Research

Internal Governance Monopoly: A Working Concept for Structural Failure in Modern Organizations

Author: Evan Micheal Foster

Platform: SSRN

Abstract ID: 6444178

This paper lays out a working explanation for why organizations keep breaking in similar ways even when the people, industry, and strategy change. Work piles up in the same places, decisions get pushed through the same bottlenecks, and the same roles keep absorbing the fallout.

It shifts the focus away from blaming execution or communication and shows how the setup itself keeps producing the same outcomes.

Beyond Financial Dominance

Author: Evan Micheal Foster

Platform: SSRN

Abstract ID: 6050034

This paper explains how financial reporting systems gradually became the main way organizations make internal decisions. Over time, that shift pulls attention, authority, and effort toward financial outcomes while other limits stay informal.

The result is not just budget pressure. It changes how work gets prioritized, how decisions are made, and where strain ends up.

Suggested Citation:
