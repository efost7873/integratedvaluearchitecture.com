Services | Integrated Value Architecture

Pick the right place to start.

By the time most people get here, they do not need another explanation of the problem. They want to know where to start, what I actually do once I am in, and whether this is a quick outside read, a defined review, or deeper work that changes how decisions and responsibility are handled.

That is what this page is for. If you want the broader picture first, go back to the homepage. If you want the short version of deliverables, go to What Clients Get.

Most people start with one of these

Some situations need a quick outside read. Some need a defined document review. Some already have work underway and want to know why it keeps dragging halfway through.

Paid advisory call

One issue in front of you. We walk through where it moves, where it stalls, and why it keeps coming back.

Fixed-fee review

Multiple issues showing up. The documents already tell the story once someone reads them the way the work actually runs.

Targeted review

A narrower entry point when you need a second opinion or want to isolate one part of the problem.

What I actually do in the review stage

I work through the documents you already use to run the place. Budgets, org charts, reporting, policy language, board material. Then I trace where decisions sit, where they stall, and where work gets sent back or rerouted.

If you want to see what comes out of that, take a look at what clients get or the common problems this work catches.

What happens if the issue goes deeper

Sometimes the review is enough. Other times it shows that the problem is built into where decisions and work are sitting. That is when the work moves into implementation.

Implementation

Decision rights shift. Work stops climbing to the wrong place. Fewer returns, fewer fixes, less wasted time.

Ongoing support

Keeps the same problems from rebuilding once pressure starts to rise again.

When the room needs alignment before anything else

Workshop

A working session to surface where the issue actually sits.

Executive or board briefing

A clear walk-through when leadership keeps circling the issue but not landing on it.

Leadership presentation

A more formal version when the room expects polish but needs reality.

What this is not

This is not project management. Not workflow cleanup. Not another layer of reporting. If you need better tracking or tools, there are people who do that well.

This work focuses on where decisions sit, where work lands, and why the same problems keep coming back. If you want the underlying structure, you can read the IVA Standard.
