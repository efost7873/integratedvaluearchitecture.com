Services

Work gets stuck when too much authority and too much work keep landing in the same place. I help organizations find where that is happening, show how it is playing out in real work, and fix the structure underneath it.

You do not need a perfect diagnosis before reaching out. Most people start because something feels harder than it should. Decisions take too long. The same founder, manager, office, or team keeps getting pulled back into cleanup. Problems that were supposedly fixed keep showing up again in slightly different form.

That usually gets treated like a communication problem, a staffing problem, or a culture problem. Sometimes those things are part of it. A lot of the time the deeper issue is that one lane has become the default gateway for what counts and what can move. Once that happens, too much work follows it.

I work on that structure. Not just naming the problem, actually changing where decisions sit, where work lands, and who keeps getting stuck carrying too much of it.

How the work usually moves

Some problems are contained enough to look at directly. Others are spread across too many roles, decisions, and workarounds to guess at cleanly.

That is why the service path is built in layers. Some clients need a fast answer on one pressure point. Some need a leadership or board conversation because people can feel the drag but do not agree on what is causing it. Some need the full sequence because the same problem has already spread far enough that patching around it is just creating more work.

What I actually do inside these services

Not software. Not project management. Not another layer people have to feed. I work on the structure underneath the mess.

Read the real structure

I review the documents, reporting lines, decision pathways, recurring choke points, and actual work patterns that show how the organization is really running, not how it says it runs.

Map where authority and work are concentrating

I identify where one founder, one office, one role, or one dominant lane has quietly become the default gateway for what counts and what can move.

Show what the bottleneck is costing

I trace where work is getting delayed, where decisions are sitting too high, where people are rewriting or rerouting work just to get it accepted, and where the same cleanup keeps landing.

Redesign decision rights and flow

I help move authority, ownership, and follow-through to the right places so fewer things stall and fewer people spend their week cleaning up avoidable nonsense.

Who this work is for

This is not limited to one size of organization. The pattern is what matters.

This work applies when the bottleneck is one founder who still has to touch too much before anything moves. It applies when a small team keeps routing everything through one person because nobody else has standing to finish the work. It applies when a nonprofit, public agency, or larger organization has one office or leadership lane carrying far more decision traffic than it should.

The issue is not how big the org chart is. The issue is whether too much authority and too much work keep landing in the same place.

What changes

The goal is not cleaner language around the same problem. The goal is that the place actually runs better.

Decisions stop sitting in the same offices waiting for approval.

Work stops getting kicked back and forth between teams.

The same people stop getting buried fixing problems that started somewhere else.

Projects stop slowing down for reasons nobody can cleanly explain.

You spend less time redoing work that should have been finished the first time.

You get usable capacity back without immediately defaulting to hiring more people.
