Services | Integrated Value Architecture

Services built around how organizations actually get stuck

Work does not pile up by accident. Decisions keep routing back to the same place, approvals stack up, and teams spend too much time cleaning up problems the structure keeps producing. This work starts by identifying where that is happening, then moves into redesign and support so the problem does not just come back wearing a different shirt.

Entry lane

Some organizations need a fast way to test whether the structure is part of the problem before they commit to larger work. That is where the entry lane comes in.

Paid advisory call

A focused conversation for leaders who need a second set of eyes on a structural problem, a decision bottleneck, or a situation that keeps creating unnecessary work.

Fixed-fee review

A document-based review that shows where decisions are getting stuck, where work is being rerouted, and where too much authority and too much work are landing in the same place.

See Fixed-Fee Review

Targeted review options

Strategic readiness review, implementation friction review, or second-opinion review for organizations that need a defined entry point around a specific structural issue.

Middle lane

Sometimes the organization already knows where the pain is. What it needs is a clearer way to frame it, present it, or work through it with leadership, boards, or key decision-makers.

Workshop

A structured working session to make a governance problem visible, clarify where work and authority are getting tangled, and move a leadership group toward a usable direction.

Executive or board briefing

A focused briefing for leadership or boards that need the problem explained in plain language without turning it into another vague discussion about culture or communication.

Leadership presentation

A presentation built around the actual structural issue, the visible consequences, and what it would take to stop the pattern from repeating.

Core lane

This is the main work. It starts with seeing where the structure is creating overload, bottlenecks, rework, and decision strain. Then it moves into redesign and support.

Diagnostic

A structured review of the organization’s existing documents to identify where authority overlaps, where work keeps bunching up, and where recurring failure is being produced by the structure itself.

Implementation

Redesign of how decisions, authority, and work are actually handled so the organization can stop relying on workarounds, escalations, and cleanup labor just to keep moving.

See Decision Rights Redesign

Maintenance and support

Ongoing support to keep the redesigned structure working, reinforce accountability, and prevent the organization from drifting back into the same failure pattern.

Side lane

Not every organization is ready for a full engagement. Some need adjacent support, a lighter advisory structure, or a way to stay close to the work without starting at full scale.

Partner network

Collaboration with adjacent professionals whose work intersects with governance, leadership, operations, risk, finance, or organizational design.

Supporter path

A way for people and organizations to support the development and spread of the work while staying connected to what is being built.

Retainer-lite advisory support

Limited ongoing access for organizations or professionals that need continued strategic support without a full redesign engagement.

Where most people start

The fixed-fee review is usually the clearest starting point because it uses the documents the organization already has and shows where the structure is already giving itself away.

It does not ask your team to stop and produce a pile of new material just to prove there is a problem they are already living with.
