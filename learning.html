Learning and Innovation Ledger

This is where the organization either adapts or repeats the same problem again.

Most organizations say they value learning. Then the same issues show up again a few weeks later.

That’s not because people aren’t paying attention. It’s because nothing is tracking what actually changed after the decision.

The discussion happens, then it disappears.

What this tracks

This ledger tracks whether anything actually changes after work moves through the system.

It shows whether problems are being resolved or repeated.

It also captures where adjustments are made and whether they hold.

What usually goes wrong

Issues get discussed, then dropped.

Teams agree to fix something, but nothing records what changed or whether it worked.

The same problem shows up again, and it gets treated like a new issue.

What changes

Adjustments get recorded and followed over time.

Decisions stay connected to what they were supposed to fix.

Repeated problems become visible instead of getting treated as isolated events.

Why it matters

Learning becomes something the organization actually does, not something it talks about.

Work improves over time instead of cycling through the same issues.
