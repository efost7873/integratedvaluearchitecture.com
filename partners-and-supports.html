Partners and Supporters

Not every relationship with this work should start the same way.

Some businesses are already close enough to the same failures that work should move both directions. Some belong near the work publicly now, even if a full partner relationship would be a stretch today. Some people simply understand the problem, want to back the work early, and want to stay close to it while it develops.

Those are three different relationships. This page keeps them separate on purpose.

Choose the relationship that actually fits

Partner Network

Real two-way fit. Built for businesses that should actually send and receive the right work.

Strategic Supporter

Credible early business affiliation. Close enough to matter without forcing a full partner relationship.

Supporter

Public backing from individuals who want to be connected to the work while it is still early.

Partner Network

This is where the fit is already real.

You are working close enough to the same structural failures that referrals should move both directions. You see work stall because decisions pile up in one place. You see projects slow down because nobody can move without another approval. You see the same person end up fixing the same problems because everything eventually lands back on their desk.

That is not casual overlap. That is usable alignment.

This relationship only works if both sides can recognize the problem and trust each other not to waste the handoff.

How you support the work

You send the situations where the problem is clearly structural. The client stuck in approval loops, the founder still holding every decision, the team fixing the same issue again because it never got handled in the right place.

How the work supports you

You receive a public partner listing, Founding Partner placement, top positioning as the network grows, and a full diagnostic credit if direct work becomes relevant.

Example: you are advising a client whose plan is solid, but nothing moves because everything still runs through one executive. That is not a planning problem anymore.

Strategic Supporter

This is for businesses that belong near the work without pretending the relationship is further along than it is.

The connection is real. The public affiliation makes sense. But a full two-way referral relationship would be forced right now.

This keeps that distinction intact.

How you support the work

You expand the visible business network around IVA and help place the work in front of organizations that are likely to recognize the problem later.

How the work supports you

You receive a public listing, Founding Strategic Supporter placement, control over how your business appears, and a partial credit toward a future diagnostic.

Example: your work keeps running into leadership or coordination issues that tools and planning are not fixing, but you are not yet in a position to treat those as referral situations.

Supporter Program

This is for people who want to back the work and stay close to it while it grows.

They have seen the same patterns. Work going up for approval and coming back down for cleanup. People sitting through training that does nothing. One reliable person quietly becoming the place everything lands.

This is not about buying a service. It is about being visibly connected to the work while it is still early.

How you support the work

You provide early backing and visible legitimacy while the work is still being built.

How the work supports you

You receive public recognition, Founding Supporter placement, and early visibility into how the work develops before it reaches a wider audience.

After joining, you will be directed to a short form to provide your listing details.

Founding listings

Founding placement lasts for the first six months and remains at the top as the network grows.

At launch, the homepage will include two rotating carousels. One will feature Partner and Strategic Supporter businesses. The other will feature Supporters. This page holds the full listings.
