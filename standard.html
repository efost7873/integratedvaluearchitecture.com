The IVA Standard

A governance architecture built for organizations carrying more than one kind of value

Most organizations can track money with precision. They are much weaker at giving operational reality, capacity limits, external impact, and learning their own formal standing.

What the standard does

The IVA Standard uses multiple ledgers so different domains of value do not have to fight for recognition inside a single financial frame. Each ledger has its own authority, evidence logic, and role in governance.

The five ledgers

Financial Ledger

Tracks money clearly without forcing every other problem to translate into dollars first.

Operational Ledger

Shows how work actually moves and where it stalls, reroutes, or gets stuck.

Capacity Ledger

Makes workload, decision load, and organizational carrying limits visible.

Externalities and Equity Ledger

Tracks impacts on people, communities, and obligations outside the narrow internal frame.

Learning and Innovation Ledger

Protects adaptation, development, and long-range capability instead of treating them as expendable.

Why this matters

When only one domain has formal standing, everything else gets treated as secondary. That is how organizations end up making financially legible decisions that create operational strain, overload staff, and weaken long-term performance.
