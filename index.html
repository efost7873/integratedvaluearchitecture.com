Approval bottlenecks. Slow decisions. Rework that keeps coming back.

Your strategic plan is probably fine. The structure underneath it is usually where things start breaking down.

Budget revisions get sent up, sent back, corrected, and sent up again. Expense reports get kicked back over a grammar fix or a single digit while the real issue sits there untouched. Purchase requests stall because the person reviewing them can reject them but cannot fix them. Staff already know what the problem is and still have to wait on somebody farther from the work.

That is not a motivation problem. It is not usually a communication problem either. It is a structure problem. Too much decision authority and too much work are sitting in the same few places, so everything has to pass through one overloaded lane before it can move.

Integrated Value Architecture helps organizations fix approval bottlenecks, slow decisions, repeated rework, and structural overload by redesigning where authority sits, who owns the work, and how decisions move. That work starts with what people are already living through, not with abstract theory and not with another round of process theater.

Frequently asked questions

Why does a strategic plan still stall after the retreat?
Because the retreat did not change who still has to approve the work, who can reject it without fixing it, or where the hard calls still go to wait.

Why does work keep getting sent back for edits?
Because approvals move up the chain while accountability for execution and correction moves down. The reviewer can reject it. The original owner still has to fix it. So the work comes back again.

Why does a budget revision or expense report keep getting sent back?
The person reviewing it can reject it but not fix it, while the person accountable for the result still does not have authority to fix it without approval. That creates repeated returns, delays, and cleanup.

What is a decision-rights map?
It shows who can approve, who can correct, who handles exceptions, and where a request actually moves when something changes. It makes approval paths and structural accountability visible instead of leaving them implied.

Is Integrated Value Architecture just strategic planning or business consulting?
No. It can sit before strategic planning, after strategic planning, or alongside consulting and finance support. It focuses on the approval paths, decision rights, and internal authority structure that often keep those other efforts from producing the results they should.

Do you work with small businesses? Yes, we explicitly list micro-entities such as small and founder-led businesses in the IVA Standard (v1.1). Integrated Value Architecture, LLC implements the standard ourselves as the easiest and best case study of how IVA works in action.

You've mentioned AI, what does AI have to do with governance? We are not a tech company, nor do we sell software or AI products. However, what we do is utilize a multi-ledger approach designed specifically to be AI friendly, ensuring the data fed into AI systems today and in the future will be accurate and complete. With the outdated single-ledger system, AI will propagate bad decisions based on limited data.
