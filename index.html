What Integrated Value Architecture Is

Integrated Value Architecture LLC is a governance architecture firm based in Springfield, Illinois, founded by Evan Micheal Foster, Founder and Principal Architect.

Integrated Value Architecture is a multi-ledger governance system that identifies and measures the structural conditions that shape organizational behavior. It replaces the single-ledger financial model that has defined performance since the industrial era. IVA distributes decision authority across functions and creates a coherent architecture for long-term performance and legitimacy.

Financial accounting systems were created for external reporting, yet they have shaped internal performance for more than a century. IVA corrects this structural imbalance by introducing a multi-ledger governance system that reflects how modern organizations create, maintain, and distribute value.

The structural mismatch IVA resolves

Organizations are asked to deliver more than financial results, yet they operate on a single-ledger model created for a different era.

Multiple demands, single ledger

Organizations are expected to deliver financial performance, operational reliability, workforce stability, community legitimacy, and future capability. A single financial ledger cannot represent these structural demands.

Finance forced to carry everything

When the financial ledger becomes the only ledger, finance becomes the clearinghouse for constraints that originate elsewhere.

How IVA works

IVA uses a three-step sequence to reveal structural conditions and align decision making.

1. Structural Scan

IVA identifies the structural conditions that shape organizational behavior.

2. Multi-ledger Diagnostic

IVA measures each ledger independently and reveals cross-ledger impacts.

3. Structural Implementation

IVA stabilizes governance architecture by assigning ownership, evidence, and decision authority across all five ledgers.
