What Integrated Value Architecture Is

IVA is a multi-ledger governance system that identifies and measures the structural conditions that shape organizational behavior.

Integrated Value Architecture is a multi-ledger governance system that identifies and measures the structural conditions that shape organizational behavior. It replaces the single-ledger financial model that has defined performance since the industrial era. IVA distributes decision authority across functions and creates a coherent architecture for long-term performance and legitimacy.

Financial accounting systems were created for external reporting, yet they have shaped internal performance for more than a century. IVA corrects this structural imbalance by introducing a multi-ledger accounting system that reflects how modern organizations create, maintain, and distribute value.

The structural mismatch IVA resolves

Organizations are asked to deliver more than financial results, yet they operate on a single-ledger model created for a different era.

Multiple demands, single ledger

Organizations are expected to deliver financial performance, operational reliability, workforce stability, community legitimacy, and future capability. A single financial ledger cannot represent these structural demands.

Financial dominance

When the financial ledger becomes the only ledger, it defines performance for every function. This creates structural distortions that no amount of effort or leadership can overcome.

Structural invisibility

Capacity limits, operational constraints, legitimacy exposures, and future capability investments remain invisible inside a single-ledger model. IVA makes these structural conditions measurable.

The Five Ledgers of IVA

Each ledger represents a distinct form of value that organizations must steward over time.

Financial Ledger

Tracks dollars, assets, liabilities, and equity.

Operational Ledger

Represents workflow, execution structure, and operational reliability.

Capacity Ledger

Measures staffing, systems, data, and infrastructure limits.

Externalities and Equity Ledger

Captures legitimacy, obligations, exposures, and external impacts.

Learning and Innovation Ledger

Represents future capability, adaptation, and knowledge creation.
