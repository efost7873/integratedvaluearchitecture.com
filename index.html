Why multi-ledger

IVA was built from a pattern I kept seeing in completely different places. Too much work and too much authority kept landing in the same few lanes, especially finance, so everything had to pass through them before it counted as real. That arrangement creates rework, delays, and strain that get blamed on people instead of the structure that set them up to fail.

That is why IVA uses a multi-ledger approach instead of forcing every decision through one dominant lane. It is also why I talk about internal governance monopolies. When one part of the organization becomes the only place with formal standing, everything else gets pushed through it whether it belongs there or not.

I founded IVA after seeing the same pattern in my roles across sales, healthcare, government, regulated environments, and national nonprofit work. Our own internal diagnostic had drastic implications for how quickly we get work done. The multi-ledger approach to governance provides value and clarity for organizations and businesses of any size, scope or complexity.

Frequently asked questions

Doesn't "architecture" cause confusion? I have thought about this repeatedly since founding IVA. Especially when a representative from Angie reached out trying to get me in touch with local homeowners looking for an architect. But what do you can someone who designs structures, foundations, blueprints, designed to hold weight and provide function? You call them an Architect.

Why does a strategic plan still stall after the retreat?
Even the best plans don't change approvals, decision rights, value domains, and escalation paths. Align the foundation with the plan and it becomes valuable and sustainable.

Why does a budget revision or expense report keep getting sent back?
The person reviewing it can reject it but not fix it, while the person accountable for the result still does not have authority to fix it without approval. That creates repeated returns, delays, and cleanup.

What is a decision-rights map?
It shows who can approve, who can correct, who handles exceptions, and where a request actually moves when something changes. It makes approval paths and structural accountability visible instead of leaving them implied.

Is Integrated Value Architecture just strategic planning or business consulting?
No. It can sit before strategic planning, after strategic planning, or alongside consulting and finance support. It focuses on the approval paths, decision rights, and internal authority structure that often keep those other efforts from producing the results they should.

Do you work with small businesses? Yes, we explicitly list micro-entities such as small and founder-led business in the IVA Standard (v1.1). Integrated Value Architecture, LLC implements the standard ourselves as the easiest and best case study of how IVA works in action.

You've mentioned AI, what does AI have to do with governance? We are not a tech company, nor do we sell software or AI products. However, what we do is utilize a multi-ledger approach designed specifically to be AI friendly, ensuring the data fed into AI systems today and in the future will be accurate and complete. With the outdated single-ledger system, AI will propogate bad decisions based on limited data.
