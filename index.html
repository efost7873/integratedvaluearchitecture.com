Work gets stuck because too much of it still has to pass through the same place.

I help organizations figure out why decisions keep dragging, why the same people keep getting pulled back into cleanup, and why work keeps piling up in the same lane, then I help redesign how authority and work are handled so the mess stops repeating.

I’ve seen work sit for days because somebody had to sign off on a decision they never should have owned in the first place. I’ve seen founders and managers spend half their time fixing bad calls that came from somewhere else. I’ve seen teams build their whole week around waiting on answers they shouldn’t need.

That usually gets blamed on communication, staffing, or culture. Sometimes those things are part of it. A lot of the time they are not the real cause. The deeper problem is that too much authority over what counts, what gets tracked, and what can move has been pushed into the same lane. Once that happens, too much work follows it.

This is structural governance work. I look at how the organization actually runs, where decisions are getting trapped, where work keeps getting kicked to the wrong people, and what has to change so the same delays, rework, and cleanup stop eating your time.

What is usually happening underneath it

The problem is not always that nobody cares or nobody can see it. A lot of the time people are naming it the whole time. The problem is that too much of the structure still has to pass through the same narrow lane.

One office, one role, one founder, one department, or one dominant lane becomes the place that decides what counts, what gets tracked, what gets reported, and what can move. Once that happens, work that should move directly starts getting translated, rerouted, delayed, or kicked upstairs first.

That is why the same people end up overloaded. That is why strong staff burn time working around bad approvals instead of doing the work. That is why the place keeps creating extra work just to preserve normal functioning.
