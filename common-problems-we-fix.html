Common Problems

These problems show up the same way in different organizations

The details change, but the pattern is familiar. Work slows down, decisions get pushed upward, and the same people keep absorbing the pressure. What looks like separate issues usually comes from the same structural setup.

Approvals keep stacking up

Decisions that should be handled at one level get pushed upward. Work waits for sign-off, then waits again when priorities shift. The backlog grows even when teams are moving as fast as they can.

The same people keep making the calls

Work keeps routing back to the same office, department, or role. Those people become the default decision-makers, even when the work does not belong there.

Everything depends on them, so everything slows down around them.

Work gets rerouted just to keep moving

Teams find ways around the structure. They escalate decisions, bypass steps, or handle work informally so it does not stall.

It works in the moment, but it creates more confusion, more rework, and less clarity over time.

Deadlines move even when effort is high

Work does not stall because people are not trying. It stalls because decisions are unclear, approval paths are overloaded, and work keeps getting redirected midstream.

Problems get explained instead of fixed

The same issues get labeled as communication problems, staffing gaps, or execution failures. Plans are adjusted. New processes are introduced.

The pattern stays because the structure has not changed.
