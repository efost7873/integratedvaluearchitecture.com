Decision Rights Redesign

Decisions stop piling up when ownership is clear

When decisions are unclear, they move upward by default. Approvals stack up, work slows down, and the same people end up carrying decisions that should have been handled elsewhere.

What decision failure looks like in practice

A team finishes the work but cannot move forward without approval. The decision sits until someone with authority has time to look at it. Then a follow-up question comes back, and the cycle repeats.

Multiply that across dozens of decisions and the structure starts producing delay as a normal outcome.

Common Problems We Fix Structural Overload Case

Why decisions keep routing upward

Authority is not clearly placed, so people escalate decisions to avoid making the wrong call. That pushes more work into fewer hands and slows everything down.

Over time, those roles become permanent bottlenecks because the structure keeps sending decisions back to them.

What changes when decision rights are clear

Decisions are handled where the knowledge already sits. Work moves forward without waiting for unnecessary approvals.

Leaders stop being default decision-makers for everything and can focus on the decisions that actually belong at their level.

Teams stop escalating routine work and start operating within a structure that supports them.
