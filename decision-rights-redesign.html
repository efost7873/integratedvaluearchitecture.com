Decision Rights Redesign

Decisions don’t pile up by accident. They pile up because of where they’re required to go.

In most organizations, decision authority is concentrated in a few places. Everything routes there.

That includes decisions those roles don’t actually need to make.

Over time, approvals stack up, response time slows down, and work starts waiting even when people are actively working.

What’s actually happening

Decisions get escalated by default. People defer instead of deciding. Work moves forward without clarity just to avoid waiting.

Then it comes back.

The same people review it again. Corrections get made. Time gets spent fixing work that already moved once.

What changes

Decision authority gets placed where the work actually happens.

Fewer decisions need escalation. Fewer approvals stack up in one place. Work doesn’t have to wait for the same people every time.

The goal isn’t to remove oversight. It’s to stop routing everything through the same bottleneck.

How this gets done

This starts by mapping where decisions currently sit using the documents your organization already relies on.

Then authority gets reassigned so decisions align with where the work is actually happening instead of where they’ve historically been routed.

That shift reduces backlog immediately and prevents the same bottlenecks from rebuilding.
