Evan Foster

Springfield, Illinois | Founder and Principal Architect, Integrated Value Architecture LLC

I’m based in Springfield, Illinois, and I run Integrated Value Architecture.

My work focuses on why decisions keep piling up in the same place, why work gets pushed downstream, and why people stay busy while deadlines still move.

I work with organizations across public, private, and nonprofit settings to figure out where authority is concentrated, where approvals overlap, and where the structure keeps creating rework, delay, and strain.

What I do

I start by reviewing the documents an organization already uses to run itself. That usually includes reporting lines, policies, workflows, committee structures, operating materials, planning documents, and other records that show how decisions are actually handled.

From there, I map where work lands, who has to approve it, where bottlenecks form, and where the same problems keep getting handed back to the same people.

Then I redesign how decisions, authority, and work are handled so everything doesn’t keep routing through the same offices, departments, or leaders.

That work can start with a paid advisory call, a fixed-fee review, or a larger engagement that moves from diagnosis into implementation and ongoing support.

Professional Background

My background includes finance, public health, government, and nonprofit leadership. Before launching Integrated Value Architecture, I worked in roles that made it very easy to see how overload gets built into the structure long before people start calling it a staffing or communication problem.

I previously served as Senior Director of Healthy Air Solutions at the American Lung Association, where I oversaw large grant portfolios and national program work. I also serve as Board Treasurer for 350 Chicago.

I hold a Master of Public Health and a Bachelor of Science in Biology from the University of Illinois Springfield and am pursuing doctoral work in public administration.
