Evan Foster — Springfield, Illinois

Governance architecture, decision structure, and why work keeps piling up.

I’m based in Springfield, Illinois and I run Integrated Value Architecture, a governance architecture firm focused on how decisions actually move through organizations.

Most of my work comes from watching where things break. Decisions stack up in the same place. Work gets pushed downstream. People stay busy but timelines still slip.

That pattern shows up across public agencies, nonprofits, and private organizations. The structure is usually the reason it keeps happening.

What I do

I analyze how decisions are made using the documents organizations already rely on. Then I show where approvals overlap, where work lands, and where everything starts backing up.

From there, I redesign how decisions are handled so they don’t all pass through the same people and the same points of failure.

After that, I stay involved to make sure it keeps working instead of drifting back.

Local context

Springfield and central Illinois are full of organizations doing real work with limited capacity. The same patterns show up here as anywhere else, just with less room to absorb the impact.

That makes the structure matter even more. When decisions pile up, you feel it quickly.
