Capacity Ledger

This is where overload becomes visible instead of getting absorbed.

Most organizations don’t have a real way to track capacity.

Work gets assigned. Deadlines get set. People adjust.

When something doesn’t get done, it gets treated as a performance issue instead of a capacity issue.

What this tracks

This ledger tracks whether the organization can actually carry the work it is assigning.

It shows where workload exceeds available time, attention, or resources.

It also shows where decisions are adding pressure faster than it can be absorbed.

What usually goes wrong

Capacity gets treated as flexible.

More work gets added without removing anything else. Deadlines stay in place even when the underlying load changes.

People compensate until they can’t.

What changes

Capacity becomes visible and trackable.

Decisions that add work stay connected to the load they create.

Tradeoffs become explicit instead of being pushed onto the same people over and over.

Why it matters

When capacity is visible, overload stops getting misdiagnosed as a people problem.

Work gets adjusted before it breaks instead of after.
