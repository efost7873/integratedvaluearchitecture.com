Capacity Ledger

Capacity is not infinite just because people keep compensating

Organizations often act like work can always be absorbed somewhere. In practice, the work lands on real people with limited time, attention, decision bandwidth, and recovery room.

What the Capacity Ledger tracks

It tracks whether the organization is asking more of teams, leaders, or operating units than they can reasonably carry. That includes workload, decision load, responsiveness demands, and the cumulative strain that builds when work keeps bunching up.

Why this matters

Overload gets misread all the time. People see delay or inconsistency and assume someone is underperforming. The reality is often simpler. The structure is sending too much work to the same places.

What changes when capacity is treated as real

Leaders can see when delays are structural instead of personal. Teams stop being blamed for carrying loads the structure should never have assigned to them. Decision rights and work distribution can then be redesigned with reality in view.
