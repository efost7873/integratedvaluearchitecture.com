About

Integrated Value Architecture was built to address problems that keep getting mislabeled

Bottlenecks, overload, repeated escalation, and delayed decisions are often treated like people problems. In many organizations, they are structural problems that have been sitting in plain view for years.

About the work

Integrated Value Architecture is a governance architecture and service model focused on where work gets stuck, where decisions actually happen, and why too much authority and too much work keep landing in the same place.

The work starts by identifying the structural pattern. Then it moves into redesign so decisions, authority, and work do not keep collapsing into the same bottlenecks.

About Evan Foster

Evan Foster is the founder of Integrated Value Architecture and is based in Springfield, Illinois. His background includes work across finance, public health, environmental health, nonprofit leadership, and governance-focused research.

That mix matters because the problems IVA addresses do not stay inside one department. They show up across operations, leadership, accountability, and institutional strain.

Why this work exists

The usual fixes often disappoint because they start too late. A new plan gets written after the structure has already pushed too much work into the same few places. A new process gets designed while the same approval bottleneck stays in place.

IVA exists to get underneath that pattern.
