About

I help organizations fix why work gets stuck, decisions drag, and the same people stay overloaded.

I review how organizations actually run, identify where decisions and work are getting stuck, and help redesign the structure so those problems stop repeating.

Why I do this work

I’ve worked in finance, public health, operations, and nonprofit leadership. Across all of those roles, I kept seeing the same pattern.

Work would pile up in the same places. Decisions would take longer than they should. The same people would stay overloaded. Problems would get “fixed” and then come back in a different form.

It was usually treated as a people problem or an execution problem. Most of the time, it wasn’t. It was the way the organization was structured.

Integrated Value Architecture came out of trying to understand that pattern clearly and build a way to fix it.

Background

My work spans private sector finance, public health systems, government, and nonprofit leadership. I’ve worked directly with large-scale programs, complex operations, and organizations under real pressure.

That experience shaped how I approach this work. It has to hold up in real organizations, not just in theory.

What makes this different

Most approaches try to improve performance within the existing structure. This work focuses on the structure itself.

Instead of adding tools, processes, or plans on top of the same system, I help identify why the system keeps producing the same problems and redesign it so those patterns stop repeating.

The work is grounded in a defined governance architecture, but it is applied directly to real organizational problems.
