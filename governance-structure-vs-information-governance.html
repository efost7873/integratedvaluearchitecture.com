Comparison

Information governance does not fix governance structure

Information governance matters. Data quality matters. Records matter. But none of that tells you where decisions are getting stuck or why work keeps routing back to the same place.

What information governance handles

Information governance focuses on how information is created, stored, accessed, protected, and used. It helps organizations manage records, compliance, retention, privacy, and data handling.

What governance structure handles

Governance structure deals with where decision authority sits, how work moves, and what happens when responsibility is unclear. It explains why approvals pile up, why teams escalate work, and why the same roles become bottlenecks.

Why the two get confused

When decisions are messy, organizations often reach for better systems, cleaner data, or tighter information rules. Those may help at the edges, but they do not resolve unclear authority or overloaded decision paths.

Better information does not automatically produce better structure.

Where Integrated Value Architecture fits

Integrated Value Architecture works on the structural side. It shows where decisions are actually made, where work is bunching up, and how authority can be redesigned so the organization does not keep sending everything through the same few nodes.
