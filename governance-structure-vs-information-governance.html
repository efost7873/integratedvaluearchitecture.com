Good Governance

Good governance is visible in how decisions actually get made

It is easy to talk about accountability in principle. It is harder to show where decisions sit, who can make them, and what happens when work hits uncertainty.

What good governance looks like in practice

Decisions are made at the right level. Approval paths are clear. Work does not need constant escalation to move. People know where authority sits and what belongs to them.

That makes accountability real instead of ceremonial.

What weak governance looks like instead

Decisions drift upward. Staff work around the process because the process slows everything down. Leaders become default sign-off points for too many things, even when those choices should not need them.

On paper the organization still looks governed. In practice it is running on delay, escalation, and cleanup.
