What to send

You do not need a polished write-up. Plain language is better.

If you email, keep it simple. Tell me what keeps getting stuck, what keeps dragging, who keeps getting pulled back into the same cleanup, or what keeps coming back even after people say it was handled. That is more useful than trying to make it sound strategic.

Useful things to mention

What is happening, how long it has been happening, where the bottleneck seems to be, who keeps getting overloaded, and whether you want a call, a focused review, or a broader conversation.

You do not need to send

A giant memo, consultant language, or a full diagnosis. If the same problem keeps repeating, that is enough to start.

What happens after you reach out

The point is to get to a cleaner next step, not create more administrative theater.

If the problem is contained, the next step may be a paid advisory call or fixed-fee review.

If the issue points to a broader structural pattern, the next step may be a larger services conversation.

If it is not the right fit, I will tell you that instead of forcing it into a category it does not belong in.
