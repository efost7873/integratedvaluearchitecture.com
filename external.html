Externalities and Equity Ledger

This is where impact actually gets recorded instead of talked about.

Most organizations say they care about impact, equity, and stakeholders. Then none of it shows up in the same place decisions are made.

Teams track financial performance in detail. They track operations closely. But when a decision affects customers, communities, or partners, it usually gets discussed once and disappears.

This ledger forces those effects to be recorded, tracked, and revisited the same way financial outcomes are.

What shows up here

Decisions that shift cost, risk, or burden outside the organization. Changes that affect access, fairness, or outcomes across different groups. Commitments made to stakeholders that actually need to be followed through.

If it affects people outside the immediate decision chain, it belongs here.

What usually goes wrong

External impact gets handled informally. Someone raises a concern in a meeting. Someone else says they’ll keep it in mind. Then the decision moves forward and nothing tracks what actually happened.

Later, when issues surface, no one can point to where the decision was made or what tradeoffs were accepted.

What this changes

External impact becomes visible, assigned, and tracked over time. Decisions that create downstream effects don’t just move on. They stay attached to real outcomes.

That doesn’t slow decisions down. It stops the same problems from coming back later with higher cost and more pressure.
