Comparison

GRC manages exposure. IVA redesigns the structure producing the strain.

Governance, risk, and compliance work is important. But it usually enters after the organization has already decided how authority, approvals, and work will be handled.

What GRC usually does well

GRC helps organizations identify risk, monitor controls, document obligations, and keep compliance responsibilities visible. It is useful when exposure, oversight, and accountability need to be tracked and maintained.

What GRC usually does not answer

It does not usually explain why approvals keep stacking up, why the same people keep becoming bottlenecks, or why teams keep rerouting work just to keep things moving.

Those are structural questions about decision rights, work flow, and authority placement.

What IVA is doing instead

Integrated Value Architecture identifies where decisions are actually being made, where work is bunching up, and where the structure keeps sending pressure to the same place.

It then redesigns how decisions, authority, and work are handled so those pressure points do not keep rebuilding.

Decision Rights Redesign Services

How the two can relate

GRC can help an organization manage the consequences of weak structure. IVA works on the structure itself. One helps monitor and govern risk. The other helps stop producing the same failure pattern in the first place.
