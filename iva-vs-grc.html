IVA vs GRC

Compliance can be strong while the system is still overloaded.

GRC systems are built to make sure organizations follow rules, manage risk, and document compliance.

They are good at that.

They are not built to fix where decisions pile up or where work keeps getting pushed back through the same points.

What GRC does

GRC systems define controls, document processes, and ensure requirements are met.

They track whether something was done correctly according to a defined standard.

They reduce risk by making sure the organization stays within those boundaries.

Where it breaks down

GRC does not change where decisions sit or how work moves.

You can have strong controls and still have decisions routed through the same bottleneck.

You can be fully compliant and still have work coming back for rework because the structure keeps creating it.

What IVA does differently

Integrated Value Architecture changes where decisions are made and how authority is distributed.

It shows where approvals overlap, where work lands, and where the same issues keep getting handed back.

Then it redesigns that structure so decisions don’t keep routing through the same place.

How they fit together

GRC can operate inside a well-structured system and perform better because decisions are clearer and work flows more cleanly.

But GRC alone does not fix structural overload.
