Fixed-Fee Review

A focused structural review for one real problem that keeps dragging, bouncing back, or landing on the same people over and over again.

This is for situations where you do not need a full engagement just to get clear on what is happening. You have a live problem. Work keeps getting stuck. A decision chain keeps dragging. One founder, one manager, one office, or one team keeps getting pulled back into the same cleanup. Everybody can feel the strain, but nobody has put a clean structural read on it yet.

That is where a fixed-fee review fits. I take a focused look at one issue, one approval chain, one recurring point of failure, or one area where the same bad pattern keeps showing up. Then I give you a direct read on what is probably driving it, where authority and work are concentrating, and what your next move should be.

This is not a generic assessment and it is not a vague brainstorming session. It is a tighter entry point into the same underlying work: finding where the structure is creating delay, rework, overload, and unnecessary drag.

When this is a fit

You usually do not need better language to know something is wrong. You need a direct read on why the same issue keeps repeating.

A routine decision still takes too many approvals and nobody can explain why.

The same founder, manager, or office keeps getting dragged back into work that should have been handled somewhere else.

Work keeps getting kicked back for changes that add time but not value.

A project, process, or recurring task feels much harder to carry than it should.

The same problem keeps showing up under slightly different names.

You need a clear next step before deciding whether a larger engagement makes sense.

What is usually going on underneath it

Different symptoms. Same structural pattern.

A lot of these situations are not random frustration. They are signs that too much authority over what counts and what can move has been pushed into the same lane. Once that happens, too much work follows it. A decision that should move directly starts waiting on the wrong people. A team that should be able to finish the work starts revising it so the default gatekeeper will accept it. The same strong staff keep burning time on cleanup and translation instead of doing the work itself.

That is why a fixed-fee review is useful. It gives you a clearer structural read on one real problem before it spreads further or gets misdiagnosed as just a people issue.

A more concrete explanation of what the issue is costing

Point to the next move

This is not just for large organizations

Small does not mean structurally healthy.

This can be useful for a founder-led business where one person still has to touch too much before anything moves. It can be useful for a small team where everything keeps climbing through one manager or one admin lane. It can be useful for a nonprofit, public agency, or larger organization where one office or leadership lane has quietly become the default gateway for too much of the system.

The issue is not size. The issue is whether too much authority and too much work keep landing in the same place.
